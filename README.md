# Eridian English Translator

> *"Grace make language tool. Tool good good good good good good good good. Question?"*

Web translator convert human verbose English → efficient Eridian English. Inspired by Rocky from Andy Weir's *Project Hail Mary*. Rocky no need articles. Rocky no need auxiliary verbs. Rocky just... communicate.

## Features

- **Third-person Rocky-speak** — Enter your name and your conversation partner's for fully personalized alien grammar.
- **Article & auxiliary verb annihilation** — "the", "a", "is", "are"? Gone. Rocky has no time for that.
- **Emphasis via repetition** — Rocky excited? Rocky *very very very very very very very* excited.
- **Question protocol** — All questions end with `, question?` as the Eridian law demands.
- **Idiom destruction** — "Break a leg"? Rocky break no leg. Rocky confused.
- **Authentic noun substitutions** — For that genuine extraterrestrial flavor.

## Usage

1. Go to **https://jai-dewani.github.io/EridianEnglish/** (browser required, Eridian chemical radio not supported yet).
2. Enter your English sentence. Any sentence. Even a weird one.
3. Optionally enter your name and your conversation partner's name for full immersion.
4. Hit **"Translate to Eridian English"**.
5. Marvel at how much of your sentence was apparently unnecessary.

## About

Rocky is a spider-like alien from Andy Weir's *Project Hail Mary* who communicates via chemical music and speaks English in the most refreshingly no-nonsense way imaginable. This translator attempts to capture that energy — minus the chemical music part, because that's a hard engineering problem.

For fans of efficient communication, charmingly broken grammar, and books that make you cry at 2am.
